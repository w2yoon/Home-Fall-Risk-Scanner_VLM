## Key excerpt: Grab Bars [§604.5]

Grab bars are required to be mounted on the walls beside and behind the water closet. They can be separate or continuous. When returned to walls, the length is measured to the centerline of the return, consistent with industry practice. When attached at other points along the bar, the length is measured to the end of the bar.

**Rear Grab Bar**: 36 inches long minimum, extends 12 inches minimum on one side of the water closet centerline and 24 inches on the other side; grab bar is 33 inches to 36 inches high measured to the top of the gripping surface.

**Side Grab Bar**: 42 inches long minimum, 12 inches maximum from the rear wall and extends 54 inches minimum in front of the water closet; the grab bar is 33 inches to 36 inches high measured to the top of the gripping surface.

The rear grab bar can be shifted to the open side of the water closet or split where it would conflict with the location of flush controls required by applicable codes (§604.5.2).

Grab bars and wall or other surfaces adjacent to them must be free of abrasive or sharp elements. Any projecting object must be located at least 12″ above grab bars and at least 1½″ from the bottom and ends of grab bars so that the reach and use of grab bars is not impeded.

### Minimum Clearances at Grab Bars

Grab bars can have circular or non-circular cross-sections but must have rounded edges. **The 1½″ clearance between the grab bar and wall is not a minimum but an absolute dimension to prevent entrapment.** Grab bars cannot rotate within fittings. The structural strength of grab bars must withstand a vertical or horizontal force of 250 lbs. at any point on the grab bar, fastener, mounting device, or supporting structure.

Cross section: circular cross section 1¼ to 2 inches in diameter, with 1½ inch clearance from the wall. Non-circular cross section with rounded edges, perimeter dimension 4 to 4.8 inches, maximum cross-section dimension of 2 inches, and clearance of 1½ inches from the wall.

**Vertical Grab Bar (ICC A117.1 Standard, not ADA)**: The ICC A117.1 Standard (referenced by the International Building Code, not the ADA Standards) requires an 18″ minimum vertical grab bar at water closets. The ADA Standards do not require this grab bar but allow a second grab bar to be within the 12″ clearance above the horizontal grab bar (§609.3) if there is a separation of 1½″ minimum.

### Toilet Paper Dispensers [§604.7]

Must be within accessible reach from the water closet. Can be located below or above (but not behind) grab bars; non-recessed dispensers must be outside the 12″ minimum high clearance above the bar yet not exceed the 48″ maximum height (measured to the outlet).

### Turning Space [§304]

Turning space is required in the room, provided as a 60″ diameter circle or T-shaped space. Doors can swing into the turning space. Elements with knee and toe clearance can overlap a portion of the turning space.

### Doors [§404]

Maneuvering clearance is required at doors based on approach and swing (48″ deep push-side, 60″ deep pull-side, per the illustrated examples in the source). No elements can overlap door maneuvering clearances.

### Water Closets in Residential Dwelling Units [§809.4]

In residential dwelling units required to be mobility accessible, at least one bathroom must comply. Accessible water closets in dwelling units are permitted several exceptions that allow some features — such as grab bars and knee/toe space below lavatories — to be provided **after construction as needed** if certain conditions are met (walls properly reinforced/blocked in advance). This exception structure (retrofit-friendly design) may be useful context for the "professional" effort tier — i.e., that even the ADA framework anticipates grab bars being added later if the wall was prepared for it.

### Common Questions excerpt — relevant to home context

"Can grab bars be mounted to the floor instead of the wall? No. The Standards require grab bars at water closets on the side and rear wall. Grab bars that are floor-mounted obstruct usable clear floor space at the water closet and may be difficult to secure."

"Is a vertical grab bar required by the Standards? No. The ADA Standards do not require a vertical grab bar at water closets. However, the ICC A117.1 standard... requires an 18″ minimum vertical grab bar on the side wall above the horizontal grab bar for people who are ambulatory."

## Note on scoping/compliance language (do not use in consumer content)

The source document extensively discusses *when* toilet rooms must comply (Title II/III scoping, new construction vs. alterations, medical care facility exceptions, etc.). This project's product must never state that a private home "complies" or "fails to comply" with the ADA, because private homes are not covered by the ADA. Only the dimensional/technical specifications above should be extracted into the knowledge base, reframed as reference figures rather than compliance requirements.
