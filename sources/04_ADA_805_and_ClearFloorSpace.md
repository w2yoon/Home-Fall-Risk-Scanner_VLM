## §805 Medical Care and Long-Term Care Facilities — full section text

**805.1 General.** Medical care facility and long-term care facility patient or resident sleeping rooms required to provide mobility features shall comply with 805.

**805.2 Turning Space.** Turning space complying with 304 shall be provided within the room.

**805.3 Clear Floor or Ground Space.** A clear floor space complying with 305 shall be provided on each side of the bed. The clear floor space shall be positioned for parallel approach to the side of the bed.

**805.4 Toilet and Bathing Rooms.** Toilet and bathing rooms that are provided as part of a patient or resident sleeping room shall comply with 603. Where provided, no fewer than one water closet, one lavatory, and one bathtub or shower shall comply with the applicable requirements of 603 through 610.

(Additional context from a related state-code guide, included for background only, not to be cited as ADA text directly: kitchens/kitchenettes in these settings, where provided, must comply with dimensional standards; notification devices for phone/door alerts are addressed for accessible sleeping rooms in some state code variants — verify against the base federal ADA Standards §805 above before using, as this particular detail may be state-code-specific rather than federal ADA.)

## Chapter 3 guide excerpt: Clear Floor or Ground Space [§305]

Sufficient clear floor or ground space is required at accessible controls, operable parts, drinking fountains, lavatories and sinks, appliances, **beds**, and other elements.

### Position [§305.5]

At most elements, clear floor or ground space can be positioned for either a forward or side approach. A side approach is typically provided or required at sales/service counters, **beds**, and most appliances.

### Surface and Size [§305.2]

The minimum size is **30″ by 48″**, applying whether positioned for forward or side approach. Clear floor spaces must be free of level changes and not slope more than 1:48. Additional space is required when the space is confined on three sides and obstructed for more than half the depth (e.g., recessed alcoves).

### Turning Space [§304]

Turning space is required in several room types, explicitly including **patient bedrooms**. Turning space can be a 60″ diameter circle or a T-shaped space (each arm/stem 36″ wide minimum, overall 60″ by 60″). Elements with knee and toe space can overlap a portion of the turning space. Doors can swing into turning space.

### Common Questions excerpt

"Is turning space required in all rooms and spaces? No. Turning space is required in certain spaces, such as toilet and bathing facilities, dressing and fitting rooms, transient lodging guest rooms [and patient bedrooms, per the room list above]... Unless addressed by a specific requirement... other spaces are not required to provide them, including lobbies, offices, and meeting rooms."

## Interpretation notes for KB authoring

- §805.3's requirement of clear floor space "on each side of the bed... positioned for parallel approach" is the direct public-domain basis for a KB item about a bed pushed against a wall / accessible from only one side. Use **30″ × 48″ minimum, per side** (from §305.2) as the reference dimension when citing this in the `detail` field.
- §304's 60″ turning space (circle or T-shape) is the reference figure for whether a wheelchair user has room to turn around in a bedroom or bathroom.
- Frame all of this to the user as: "This is the space standard written for resident bedrooms in licensed long-term care facilities — not a requirement for private homes, but a useful benchmark for the same population's needs," never as a compliance/violation claim.
